Wardrobe Links

This is a living document; if you find a new link (or find one of these is dead), please contact the Webminister to have it added (or removed).

We hope that the following links will provide useful information on garb.

Basics & Accessories

  • Cynthia Virtue: The go-to site for a wide range of topics, including hats, accessories, fabric choices, basic garb and a tunic worksheet.
  • Stephan's Florilegium: A massive collection of articles on every possible topic.
  • Barony of Politarchopolis: Has a brief article covering what -not- to wear; many links below.
  • Citadel of the Southern Pass: An article on what we consider an 'attempt at pre-17th C. Clothing', and how to scrounge from your own closet.
  • Rosalie's Medieval Woman: A terrific resource on the components of garb making; eyelets, sewing techniques, period dye colours, embellishments, etc.
  • Sidney Eileen: Hand-sewing tutorial.
  • Reconstructing History: This is a commercial site that sells patters, including for larger sizes.
  • Kostym: A broad collection of patterns and information for various eras and cultures. Most of it is in Czech.
  • Ball Buttons: Just what it says, a tutorial by Dunstana Talana the Violet on how to make ball buttons.
  • The Glove Website: The history, patterns, and technique behind making gloves.
  • Skjoldehamn Hood: A useful and easy to sew accessory.
  • Katafalk: 14th and 16th C. German accessories and clothing, including frilled veils, hoods, st Birgitta's cap, and her rendition of the Lengberg brasserie.
    •

    Early Period/Viking Era

  • Viking Answer Lady: An extensive collection of research and articles on all things Norse.
  • Midgard: While this is a LARP site, they have patterns for a good set of early european garb.
  • Project Broad Axe: Viking era clothing and accessories.
  • Hurstwic: Norse/Anglo-saxon articles and patterns.
  • Shelagh: More Norse/Anglo-Saxon clothing, including an excellent Thorsberg trouser pattern.
    •

    High-to-Late Middle Ages

  • Eva's Page: 12th and 14th century research and patterns, some in english, some in Swiss.Go to costume gallery, click on the links for research and patterns. She's a university level professor in History and Fashion History.
  • Dame Helen: 14th Century clothing resources, including Cotehardies and Houpelandes and an excellent basic suit.
  • La Cote Simple: 14th and 15th Century clothing articles and tutorials, including marshal garments.
    •

    Renaissance, Elizabethan and Landsknecht, Oh My!

  • Briana's Clothing Page: Italian Renaissance articles.
  • Renaissance Italy: Venetian Renaissance dress diaries and galleries.
  • Farthingale Calculator: Plug in the numbers, and it will tell you how wide your hoop circumfrance should be, and how they should be spaced apart. Works in inches.
  • Farthingales Corset Making Supplies: A commercial site with a wealth of tips & tricks articles for free.
  • The German Renaissance of Genoveva: 16th C. German patterns and instructions.
  • Clothing of the Low Countries: Flemish and Netherlandish clothing, 15th-16th C.
  • Spanish Seamstress: 15th-16th C. Spanish costuming (and other) topics. Excellent list of resource links.
  • Elizabethan Costume Page: Extensive information on Elizabethan era clothing, from introductory topics through pattern drafting.
  • An Elizabethan Shirt: A PDF download of Grace Gamble's instruction booklet.
  • Honor Before Victory: Landsknecht garb overview and links.
    •

    Non-European

  • Miriam's Middle Eastern Reasearch Blog: A site dedicated to the Islamic Middle East, including Ottoman, Persian, Coptic, Mamluk, and Al-Andalus.
  • The Kosode: Information and patterns on period-appropriate Japanese garb.
