Recipe Links

This is a living document; if you find a new link (or find one of these is dead), please contact the Webminister to have it added (or removed).

We hope that the following links will provide useful information on period food and cooking.

Websites & Collections

  • Gode Cookery: The go-to site for period culinary inspiration.
  • Stephan's Florilegium: A massive collection of articles on every possible topic.
  • Hildegarde's Kitchen: With information on Potluck recpies, and some feast menus.
  • Medieval York: Eulalia Hath a Blogge: Contains more potluck suggestions, information on period beverages, and information on period spices amongst other things.
  • Dame Helen: Contains advice on how to cook a feast, even more potluck suggestions, and an article on 14th C. Northern European food.
  • The Aethelmearc Gazette: An article on Landsknecht cooking.
  • Lady Aibell ingen Dairmata: A wealth of links and information on food.
  • Pass the Garum: A selection of Roman recipes.
  • Euriol's Culinary Journey: All kinds of redacted recipes, broken down by region, category and time period.
  • Medieval Cookery: More redacted recipes, including a listing of easy to cook ones.

Books

  • The Book of Cooking in Maghreb and Andalus in the era of Almohads: A PDF download of a 13th century Andalusian cookbook, author unknown.
  • The Forme of Cury: a 14th C. English cookbook.
  • Le Viandier de Taillevent: a 14th/15th C. French-language cookbook.
  • Harleian MS 279: redactions of a 15th C. English cookbook.
  • Liber de Coquina: a 14th C. French and Italian cookbook.
  • Apicius: an ongoing translation of a 1st C. Roman text.
  • Coquinaria: hosts two dutch cookbooks; Wel ende edelike spijse (15th C) and The convolute Gent KANTL 15 (16th C).
  • How to Milk and Almond, Stuff and Egg, and Armour a Turnip: A thousand years of recipes by David Friedman and Elizabeth Cook. This is a PDF download of the full book.
  • Cariadoc's Miscellany: An online version of Cariadoc's book; the recipe's chapter is extensive.
